  • MARA

    Artificial Intelligence Project


    BRIEF

    This project collaborated with Samsung and aim to identify the role of mobile devices and changes of mobile lifestyle in the era of AI .Propose compelling Product concepts for next mobile reflecting on this AI innovation and make human more like Super Hero.







    What is MARA?


    “Mara”is an AI that is friendly, caring and smart assistant, that helps you anytime, anywhere to prevent unexpected situations as well as providing quick advice and emotional support. Mara is able do this by understanding the user, not only through big data, but also your emotions, personality and your value. This will make Mara empathise the users and truly give you the best support.









    THE DESIGN PROCESS
    The current AI

    What is the Current AI out there? We have divided into 4 parts; Personal Assistant, Expert Intelligence, Home assistant and Entertainment.






    The growth of AI

    As of June 2016, artificial intelligence received $974m of funding. This year’s funding is set to surpass 2015’s total and CB Insights suggests that 200 AI-focused companies have raised nearly $1.5 billion in equity funding.





    The barrier of AI

    Despite the increases AI market it seems like not many people are not using the AI or satisfy of what the AI is providing. From our interview we found that most people only use alarm clock or asking for weather and calls. They do not seems to use any other feature despite it can do more.







    The surprised moment

    During our interviews and observing user’s daily routine we found, people meets different kinds of surprises and sometimes, they cause diffculties and problems. And a lot of people find it is diffcult to cope with negative experiences, where we think that it is an opportunity for AI to help.



    Workshop

    In order to further understand people are in this kind of situations we decided to do workshop and interview and how do they behaviour and feel in this situations.


    Insights

    We have found 3 main insight during the workshop. First people have di erent sets of value, whether that is towards work or relationship. Second because of this value, people have different needs. Lastly there are 4 elements people also judge when a situations happened.















    4 Elements of Motivations

    There are 4 elements people always think about when judging a situations. That is “capability of solving the problem”, “Effects on life cycle”, “Impact on relationships”, and “Impact on resources”




    Ideation
    According to all the insights, we started to design the AI system and storyboard, as well as the new device.


    “Mara” is a personal voice assistant that are able to preventing unexpected situations, provide quick advice and give you emotional support. Mara is able do this by understanding the user, not only through big data, but also your emotions, personality and your value. This will make Mara empathise the users and truly give you the best support.







    How does it work?

    MARA is divided mainly between Quick advice and Emotional support. It will extract informations not only from your data, but also emotions, personality, person value and etc. To provide the way it quick advice and support. This could be the word ,tone, or the order of how AI interact with the users.

  • Take It Easy

    Luggage for wheelchair users


    BRIEF

    “Take It Easy” is a suitcase designed for wheelchair users. It solves the problems of inconvenient use and psychological pressure of wheelchair users with suitcases on the market, enabling wheelchair users to pack their luggage on their own, without assistances of other people.








    THE DESIGN PROCESS


    Research & Interview

    We have done a lot of interview with wheelchair users. From the interview, we noticed that wheelchair users met some problems with current suitcases while packing up their luggage. They couldn’t use the current suitcases without other people’s help, therefore they can not barely travel by themselves.





    As a summary of target's users' journey based on a great deal of interview, we simply mapped out the touchpoint in each step.





    Furthermore, we have done the User Experience Journey Map





    Intergrate in 7 Problems



    Summarise in 2 Key Problems


    "WEIGHT"


    A suitcase filled with luggage is too heavy for them to take down from the table. They need to asked for help. Moreover, if the suitcase lled wtih stuff, they certainly can not put on the table as well.

    "HEIGHT"


    Current suitcases should be lay flat while using. However, a wheelchair user can’t reach the suitcases for the limit of their hands. The suitcase should be placed on a small table by other people for them to pack up luggage.





    Brainstorming and Sketch

    Depending on two main problems, we started to brainstorm the solution via variety of Design Method, for example: Morpholoical Charts.






    Marketing Research

    Having done a lot of research of suitcases, compared with the material, price and the size. It would be helpful for designing the sharp and the function when we sketch.







    Sketch

    According to our key problems, we had sketched over hundards of paper and we tried to make the wheelchair users operated our suitcase as easy as normal. Meanwhile, we have made lots of prototypes to test our ideas.








    Take It Easy
    Modern people like to enjoy lives through traveling, however, a normal activity like travel would be luxury to people using wheelchairs. They will meet barriers not only in the environment, but also their psychological conditions. They would feel bad when always asking other people for help since they don’t want to become a burden to them.

    From the basis of “ design for wheelchair traveling”, we searched for the touch point of design. We hope that through our design, wheelchair users can travel with carefree and satisfaction independently. Last but not the least, we’d like to expand our design to universal design. We hope our design can be benefit not only for the wheelchair users but also old people or people who move with difficulty. We believe that not only the environment, but also products should be accessible to everyone.






    New Scenario Video


    Main Features


    1. DETACHABLE DESIGN

    The whole design is a combination of two detachable cases. Users can pack up their luggage of commonly use on upper case for special condition without open the whole suitcase. The detachable design also benefits users with flexible size depending on their days of traveling.

    The switch design on the upper case can detach the combination, but it can hardly move without applying a force for safety consideration and accident. Moreover, there is a touch-avoiding design for additional protection.





    2. UPRIGHT LID DESIGN

    The innovative design of upright lid enables users to use the suitcase without moving it, solving the ergonomic problem of height and weight. Wheelchair users can easily use the suitcase by themselves with one hand beside the wheelchair.





    3. INNER BAGS PACKING

    The interior design of suitcase combines with inner bags, helping users distribute the weight of luggage and reach the object in the bottom. The design solves the height and weight limit problems of wheelchair users. The inner bags are designed with reaching rope connected to the lid of lower case, user can easily use with one hand.


    Inner bags design is based on the using habits of our targeted user, female wheelchair users. Inner bags help users to distribute the luggage weight and assorting the luggage while packing.




    New Scenario





    Drop Avoiding Bag

    Drop-avoiding bag is a design in the edge of lower case, preventing the object from falling into the bottom but hard to take. When the user can hardly bend down to take the falling object, they can pull the edge of the drop-avoiding bag that they can reach them.

  • Diversified Cam

    Camera for multiple users


    BRIEF

    In the market, there are few products which are considered to be designed for single and multi-users. We have to consider all users are how to communicate and how to deliver the detailed message to other users.





    THE TOPIC I CHOOSE

    When you are alone, then you want to take a photo of yourself. You need help from others. If the helper is a beginner, it is hard to explain what you want. For instance, the angle of taking photo, proportion and the distance. General digital camera did not come up with good solution within this situation. So I decided to dig into this problem.




    THE DESIGN PROCESS

    Research & Interview

    First of all, I interview a lot of target users and write down all the pain points. We are able to separate into two main categories: Problems for Single Users and Multi-Users.





    Print out all the issues in a paper and cut it in a small pieces so as to intergrate them.
    Eventually, giving a title to different groups and analyise them.





    Summerise Key Insights

    According to the issues, I classify issues under 8 topics and find out the key insights from all of the topics, therefore, I got the interesting opportunity for this project:

    "A do not know how to tell B what the photo style he wants"



    Sketch and Design

    From all of the issues, finally I decided my topic that is to separate the "Preview screen" and "Camera lens". It is because it is the most directly way to preview accuratly than communicate to the helper even if the helper is a beginner. if you can preview it, the photo would be what you want.







    Diversified Cam


    The way to solve problem is to separate the “preview screen” and “camera lens”. The advantage of this design is you can preview all the details by yourself accurately. Merely Requesting the helper oriented the camera lens to you.

  • KeyFrame

    Interactive Design



    BRIEF

    I have participated in Mind the Gap exhibition in London Design Festival 2013. Mind the Gap looks at the challenges facing modern urban transportation hubs and the design strategies used to respond to them. This collaborative project between two groups of design students – RCA Design Products' Platform 17 and the National Cheng Kung University in Tainan, Taiwan – focused on London’s Marylebone and Tainan train stations.




    INTRODUCTION

    KeyFrame–Tainan is an interactive device designed by Long-Kei Fong, Yuan-Chi Tseng and Yu-Chiao Wang that combines culture, digital arts, local music and technology to bring the viewer into Tainan life. Designers mix the style of picture book, watercolor brushes and other visual effects to create a digital scroll representing the urban image of Tainan.

    The horizontal scroll captures the local culture, friendly and laid-back residents and the landscape of the ancient capital of Taiwan, Tainan. The viewer is an urban wanderer who can even jump from real world into the virtual urban daily life by the Kinect technology. S/he can follow her/himself and others to explore the illustrated city step by step.

  • Smooth Start

    Service Design



    CHALLENGE

    Getting a house, setting up a bank account, finding a phone and wi-fi. It’s a huge headache when you come to a new city.




    CONCEPT


    Information Flow

    We offer our services to universities that sign up with a one-off joining fee.

    Their students are offered the opportunity to register with Smooth Start online when they receive conformation of their place. If they choose to sign up, the university simply passes the student information to Smooth Start.

    Smooth Start gives this information directly to its partners: the accommodation company, the bank and the telephone provider, who set up temporary accommodation, a bank account, and a mobile phone for the student, depending on their needs. The service providers feed the students’ new bank account details, address and telephone number back to Smooth Start and welcome their new international customer to their service.




    Values for stakderholders

    By using Smooth Start, students, who have already established a relationship of trust with their university, will feel like their university is working together with them to give them the best possible experience of arriving in a new country.




    By using Smooth Start, the universities will be able to provide a valuable and surprisingly easy experience for students, whilst reducing their workload at the beginning of the term.






    By partnering with Smooth Start, the banks, housing companies and phone network providers will have access to a sizeable and lucrative market share whilst also reducing their administrative burden in processing and checking documents.

    RBS High Street Ken branch gave us insight into the fact that banks don’t see the value in international students because they take a lot of staff time, have diffculties with communication and don’t always come prepared - a service like Smooth Start would reduce in-branch time and prepare the students better for their new start. Smooth Start can share these details back to the other service providers and the university.

    The university can now better keep their information up-to-date. By grouping all the university’s students together through Smooth Start, we’re also increasing our buying power, and getting the best deals for the students on these essential services.
