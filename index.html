  • YONA

    Service Design - Future of Music Education




    Helping anyone wanting to learn how to play their favourite songs in a fun and accessible way.

    1 minutes to understand YONA




    Context

    Music is a beautiful and common language in the world, everyone can understand but few people can speak. As a pianist and have learned piano for over 15 years, I realised a fact that, for a lot of people, learning music always looks difficult and take a long time before they can enjoy it, but learning music is not as hard as people think.

    In this context, I worked with Royal College of Music and found out an accessible way of learning music for the next generation to meet their changing need, with ultimate purpose of making more people enjoy playing music.







    Research & Interview

    As part of the discover stage, my resarech involved ethnographic interviews with 38 people, including 27 music students, 5 music professionals, 4 music teachers, 2 parents, it also involved 1 workshop, online survey with over 100 responses, 2 studnet clubs observation and 1 music-tech company consultant.






    What I understand

    By mapping out the "cycle jounrey of learning a song" from the research, there are a lot of repeating practice cycles in each section and it was taken a long time to learn. But it was interesting that the mixed emotions (positive and negative) were occurred throughout the learning journey.


    Compared different level of learning cycle, for the beginner, long learning curve in Technical Section lead to loss of interest easily and hard to keep self-encouragement.








    Future Tool - Augmented Reality Glasses



    In 2016, Venture capitals and corporate investors pouring $2.3 billion into VR/AR startups. Facebook believe that AR smart glasses will go mainstream from 2022, in the next generation, we would consume AR glasses, where Google glasses got it wrong now, because they try to put it in social situation, but I believe it is a huge potential on education and training, where AR glasses put information in an engaging and visual way.








    Key Challenges & Insights

    Many beginners are easy to give up due to the long learning time at early stage, as well as mismatching their initial goals. This 3 Key challenges were translated into the following opportunities:

    1. Making short-term achievement at early stage

    2. Goal-orientation drives learning

    3. Sociability arouse motivation and continuance in learning music






    How might we help self-taught learner being able to play their favourite song and learning music knowledge in an accessible and supportive environment by goal-oriented practice?




    The Solution

    YONA is a Augmented Reality music learning platform engaging everyone to enjoy playing their favourite song in a fun and easy way.




    YONA change the learning system how beginners learn piano - Using your passion to reach musical mastery. There are 3 key parts for YONA:

    1. Passion-Driven Learning
    - Using your favourite song to inform you what you need to work on

    2. On-instrument Learning Expierence
    - Earning quick achievement by AR glasses

    3. Musical Social Platform in AR World
    - Providing accessible online and offline meet-up platform






    1. Passion-Driven Learning - 3 LEVELS

    Level 1 create quick engagement by following simple instruction on instrument; Level 2 provide tailored missions based on your favourite song and your current level, so you can understand exactly what you have to learn and improve to reach your goal. You can also just focus on exploring the sections that you find most interesting, that is Level 3.








    2. On-istrument Learning Experience - AR

    AR glasses is a huge potential tool on education, which create a quick engagement on playing music before a long practice time and reading the music note by on-instrument instruction. I tested this idea in exhibition and it was increadible success. (watch previous video)







    3. Musical Social Platform

    YONA support people to meet their learning partner with similar level and same interest by a customised choice, providing a online and offline platform to increase people's self-motvation and continuance.







    Information Architecture


    The outcome

    “YONA gives information in a easy way than we see before, you have a good framework. This is how you can engage people in different way. That is what is new, that is not being done yet.”

    George Waddell -- Research Associate in Performance Science, Royal College of Music

  • Take It Easy

    Luggage for wheelchair users




    Designing a suitcase for wheelchair users being confident to travel by themselves.


    “Take It Easy” is a suitcase designed for wheelchair users. It solves the problems of inconvenient use and psychological pressure of wheelchair users with suitcases on the market, enabling wheelchair users to pack their luggage on their own, without assistances of other people.






    Research & Interview

    We have done a lot of interview with wheelchair users. From the interview, we noticed that wheelchair users met some problems with current suitcases while packing up their luggage. They couldn’t use the current suitcases without other people’s help, therefore they can not barely travel by themselves. There is no specialised suitcase designed for wheelchair uers in the market.





    Wheelchair users always want to take care of themselves if possible. But from the past scenario, it was clear that wheelchair users met a lot of problmes on using their suitcase becasue of the unsuitable design.







    Intergrate in 7 Problems



    Summarise in 2 Key Problems

    "WEIGHT"


    A suitcase filled with luggage is too heavy for them to take down from the table. They need to asked for help. Moreover, if the suitcase lled wtih stuff, they certainly can not put on the table as well.

    "HEIGHT"


    Current suitcases should be lay flat while using. However, a wheelchair user can’t reach the suitcases for the limit of their hands. The suitcase should be placed on a small table by other people for them to pack up luggage.



    ------

    Brainstorming and Sketch

    Depending on two main problems, we started to brainstorm the solution via variety of Design Method, for example: Morpholoical Charts.






    Marketing Research



    Having done a lot of marketing research of suitcases, learned from the material, manufacture and structure. Our design should be light, universal and non-disable appearance.







    Sketch

    According to our key problems, we had sketched over hundards of paper and we tried to make the wheelchair users operated our suitcase as easy as normal. Meanwhile, we have made lots of prototypes to test our ideas.






    The Solution


    Take It Easy
    Modern people like to enjoy lives through traveling, however, a normal activity like travel would be luxury to people using wheelchairs. They will meet barriers not only in the environment, but also their psychological conditions. They would feel bad when always asking other people for help since they don’t want to become a burden to them.

    From the basis of “ design for wheelchair traveling”, we searched for the touch point of design. We hope that through our design, wheelchair users can travel with carefree and satisfaction independently. Last but not the least, we’d like to expand our design to universal design. We hope our design can be benefit not only for the wheelchair users but also old people or people who move with difficulty. We believe that not only the environment, but also products should be accessible to everyone.






    New Scenario Video


    Main Features


    1. DETACHABLE DESIGN

    The whole design is a combination of two detachable cases. Users can pack up their luggage of commonly use on upper case for special condition without open the whole suitcase. The detachable design also benefits users with flexible size depending on their days of traveling.

    The switch design on the upper case can detach the combination, but it can hardly move without applying a force for safety consideration and accident. Moreover, there is a touch-avoiding design for additional protection.





    2. UPRIGHT LID DESIGN

    The innovative design of upright lid enables users to use the suitcase without moving it, solving the ergonomic problem of height and weight. Wheelchair users can easily use the suitcase by themselves with one hand beside the wheelchair.





    3. INNER BAGS PACKING

    The interior design of suitcase combines with inner bags, helping users distribute the weight of luggage and reach the object in the bottom. The design solves the height and weight limit problems of wheelchair users. The inner bags are designed with reaching rope connected to the lid of lower case, user can easily use with one hand.


    Inner bags design is based on the using habits of our targeted user, female wheelchair users. Inner bags help users to distribute the luggage weight and assorting the luggage while packing.




    New Scenario





    Drop Avoiding Bag

    Drop-avoiding bag is a design in the edge of lower case, preventing the object from falling into the bottom but hard to take. When the user can hardly bend down to take the falling object, they can pull the edge of the drop-avoiding bag that they can reach them.

  • Diversified Cam

    Camera for multiple users




    Designing a camera for multi-users, to establish well-communication system with user-friendly manipulation


    Brief

    In the market, there are few products which are considered to be designed for single and multi-users. We have to consider all users how to communicate and how to deliver the detailed message to other users.






    My approach and reframing

    Along with the increasing number of solo-travellers, I believe the camera design should be different for the new user behaviour. solo-travellers always take photo by themselves, sometimes they might need other people's help, but it is hard to take the perfect angle as you want due to the quick and non-effective communication.





    Research & Interview

    After finished ethnographic interview and listed out all the problems, I found that taking photos by/for others always lead to frustration for both individuals.









    Summerise Key Insights

    Analysing each detail in the process, the problems always happen in language barriers and complicated manipulation, which causes embarrassing communication.







    Sketch and Design

    The initial idea is to separate the "Preview screen" and "Camera lens". The camera owner will be able to preview the composition and clearly communicate to the helper through other system.





    The Solution


    Diversified Cam


    The way to solve problem is to separate the “preview screen” and “camera lens” physically. The advantage of this design is that you can handle all the details by yourself accurately, as the helper simply follow your instruction through the camera lens.

    In order to solving the problem of complicated operation, Minimalism design was applied in the form of the camera and button design, as well as giving a modern style and neat in appearance.

This will make Mara empathise the users and truly give you the best support.

From our interview we found that most people only use alarm clock or asking for weather and calls. They do not seems to use any other feature despite it can do more.

And a lot of people find it is diffcult to cope with negative experiences, where we think that it is an opportunity for AI to help.

This will make Mara empathise the users and truly give you the best support.

It will extract informations not only from your data, but also emotions, personality, person value and etc. To provide the way it quick advice and support.   • Health Cadet

    Service Design - Healthcare Project




    How might we enrich interconnections within the community to encourage people to take responsibility for their health?

    This project is awarded with THIPP Vanguard Staff Innovation Fund (£25,000) from Tower Hamlets Integrated Provider Partnership to run a pilot scheme in William Davis primary school and Blithehale Medical Centre.



    The backdrop

    The multi-culturally diverse and relatively poor borough of Tower Hamlets faces different challenges. The practice has little means to deal with the root causes of the increase in people's demand. The health center is therefore looking for a new way of transfering knowledge utilising it's resource with the community.







    Research & Interview

    The research involved demographic interview, local media analysis, observation in centre and lots of patient interview. It was clear that patient always feels dismissed by the doctor and questions the efficacy of his advice, there is no feedback loop in healthcare centre and they are disconnected with doctors.








    Summerise Key Insights
    We combinated all of the insights from our different research to make a big picture, we concluded them as our problem statement: "There is an increasing demand for healthcare and the practice has little PROBLEM means to deal with the root causes of people's demand." It also can be divided in the following 4 key problems:





    Ideation

    Generate different ideas via variety of design method and discussion




    The Solution


    Health Cadet



    Concept

    Health Cadets builds on the idea of the existing after school club. With the ultimate goal of keeping people healthier and reducing demand for care, it aims to be more strategic about what it teaches and how those learnings can be transferred back to the community.

    By teaching on local health issues with tools to make the learning outcomes of each lesson highly visible, the after school club can become an effective health promotion tool in the community.





    Health Cadets uses children aged between 12 and 16 as a method of transferring knowledge of preventative healthcare into the community. So the service starts here with the NHS who orchestrate it. It starts with a toolkit.





    ToolBox
    Within the toolkit there is everything the practice needs to teach children: ID badges for kids, Achievement books, work folders, lesson plans and certificates. It is also creating more meaningful feedback from kids and community.

  • KeyFrame

    Interactive Design





    London Design Festival - Interactive Installation

    I have participated in Mind the Gap exhibition in London Design Festival 2013. Mind the Gap looks at the challenges facing modern urban transportation hubs and the design strategies used to respond to them. This collaborative project between two groups of design students – RCA Design Products' Platform 17 and the National Cheng Kung University in Tainan, Taiwan – focused on London’s Marylebone and Tainan train stations.




    INTRODUCTION

    KeyFrame–Tainan is an interactive device designed by Long-Kei Fong, Yuan-Chi Tseng and Yu-Chiao Wang that combines culture, digital arts, local music and technology to bring the viewer into Tainan life. Designers mix the style of picture book, watercolor brushes and other visual effects to create a digital scroll representing the urban image of Tainan.

    The horizontal scroll captures the local culture, friendly and laid-back residents and the landscape of the ancient capital of Taiwan, Tainan. The viewer is an urban wanderer who can even jump from real world into the virtual urban daily life by the Kinect technology. S/he can follow her/himself and others to explore the illustrated city step by step.

  • Worth+

    Service Design - Future Enterprise




    Brining Sustainability Practices to the Field of Accountancy

    In September of 2015, world leaders at the United Nations adopted the Agenda for Sustainable Development Goals. These goals aim to end poverty, fight inequality and injustice, and tackle climate change by 2030. The Institute of Chartered Accountants of England and Whales (ICAEW) has committed to helping the UN meet these goals, and asked the Royal College of Art and Cambridge Business School to help them understand how their members could contribute.





    Worth+ Story & Concept

    Worth+ is an initiative at ICAEW that explores new ways of assigning value to actions and policies in business practices that help meet the Sustainable Development Goals.





    The backdrop





    Reserach & Insights

    Workshop wih ICAEW accountants

    We focused on stakeholders that we thought were key to helping the ICAEW assist with the Sustainable Development Goals. That includes their own members, the workforce of both accountants, their clients, consumer trends, and experts in areas related to sustainable development goals. We held three workshops, where we spoke to these stakeholders and gathered insights. We learned that ICAEW accountants have succeeded using traditional accounting methods which focus on measuring, ssuring and disclosing financial information, but like many industries were facing challenges due to advances in aritificial intelligence and the changing landscape of business.





    New Business Landscape




    The Solution

    We found that ICAEW members were unable to meet their clients' existing sustainability audit needs, which are increasing due to workforce and consumer trends. We created a way for accountants to access relevant case studies that measured progress towards the Sustainable Development Goals. The method in which case studies were identified and developed, would also make the ICAEW a leader in the field of sustainability. (concept video on top)

    The feedback
    When presented with consumer, workforce and corporate trends that showed a need to audit social responsibility and sustainability, ICAEW members became interested in exploring how they could help. ICAEW members agreed that our solution, which focuses on learning and easily finding information, was the key issue that they had overlooked to both contributing to the Sustainable Development Goals. They believed our solution would be easy for them to use, would help them provide new services for their clients leading to new revenue streams.





    Exhibition of work - Fringe Festival in 2017

    The outcome of this project raised awareness of the credibility of sustainable accounting practices within the ICAEW and requested that was selected by Imperial College and the Royal College of Art to exhibit Fringe Festival in 2017.

  • Smooth Start

    Service Design




    Helping international student get set-up in a new city, thereby establishing a trust data networking with school

    Getting a house, setting up a bank account, finding a phone and wi-fi. It’s a huge headache when you come to a new city.




    CONCEPT


    The Solution

    Information Flow

    We offer our services to universities that sign up with a one-off joining fee.

    Their students are offered the opportunity to register with Smooth Start online when they receive conformation of their place. If they choose to sign up, the university simply passes the student information to Smooth Start.

    Smooth Start gives this information directly to its partners: the accommodation company, the bank and the telephone provider, who set up temporary accommodation, a bank account, and a mobile phone for the student, depending on their needs. The service providers feed the students’ new bank account details, address and telephone number back to Smooth Start and welcome their new international customer to their service.




    Values for stakderholders

    By using Smooth Start, students, who have already established a relationship of trust with their university, will feel like their university is working together with them to give them the best possible experience of arriving in a new country.




    By using Smooth Start, the universities will be able to provide a valuable and surprisingly easy experience for students, whilst reducing their workload at the beginning of the term.






    By partnering with Smooth Start, the banks, housing companies and phone network providers will have access to a sizeable and lucrative market share whilst also reducing their administrative burden in processing and checking documents.

    RBS High Street Ken branch gave us insight into the fact that banks don’t see the value in international students because they take a lot of staff time, have diffculties with communication and don’t always come prepared - a service like Smooth Start would reduce in-branch time and prepare the students better for their new start. Smooth Start can share these details back to the other service providers and the university.

    The university can now better keep their information up-to-date. By grouping all the university’s students together through Smooth Start, we’re also increasing our buying power, and getting the best deals for the students on these essential services.
